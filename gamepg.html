GAMING / ESPORTS REGISTRATION

Esports, short for electronic sports, is a form of competition using video games. Esports often takes the form of organized, multiplayer video game competitions, particularly between professional players, individually or as teams.

Multiplayer competitions were long a part of video game culture but were largely between amateurs until the late 2000s, when the advent of streaming platforms like YouTube and Twitch enabled professional gamers to rise.

By the 2010s, esports had become a major part of the video game industry, with developers designing and funding tournaments. Esports first gained popularity in East Asia, especially in China and South Korea, later expanding worldwide.

Popular game genres include MOBA, FPS, RTS, and fighting games. Major franchises include League of Legends, Dota 2, Counter-Strike, Valorant, Overwatch, Street Fighter, Super Smash Bros., and StarCraft.

Esports continues to grow, attracting millions of viewers globally and generating billions in revenue.
